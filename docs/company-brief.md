# Sploot Company Brief

## Mission
Reduce preventable dog rehoming by screening readiness, improving fit, and supporting ownership after placement.

## Product Arc
1. Screen readiness.
2. Curate pairings.
3. Support the relationship.

## Scope
- Dogs only in v1.
- Web-first proof of concept.
- Guidance and coordination, not veterinary or training care delivery.

## Tone
- Warm
- Credible
- Non-judgmental
- Preparation-first

## Research Principles
- Human-side readiness matters more than aesthetics.
- Eye-contact-forward imagery improves emotional legibility and should be favored in brand and match cards.
- Expectation gaps, budget strain, moves, and household friction are explicit risk factors.
- The first 30 to 90 days after adoption are product-critical, not a postscript.

## Target Audience
- Younger-skewing U.S. dog owners and adopters, especially Millennial-heavy households.
- Mid/high-income but still cost-conscious consumers.
- Family and home-oriented households, not just dense urban renters.
- Mainstream users, not only early adopters.
